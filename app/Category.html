Birds

Hundreds of species of birds live in the Chesapeake Bay watershed. Some birds live here year-round, while others migrate to the Bay region to feed or nest.

Birds are some fo the region's most beautfiul - but vulnerable - species. Although each bird has distinct behaviors and habitat needs, they all serve as important links in the Bay food web. However, birds are sensitive to pollution and habitat degradation.

Click on the images below to learn about some of the birds that live in the Chesapeake Bay region.

Wild Turkey
Meleagris gallopavo

The wild turkey is a large, dark, ground-dwelling bird with a long neck and a fan-shaped tail. It lives in open, wooded areas throughout the Chesapeake Bay watershed.
